# Conversational Thai course overview

Noklingo Course 1 is a listening-first path for an English-speaking beginner who
wants useful Thai with family, during annual Thailand trips, and in ordinary
social situations. Thai script is retained as optional reference data. No lesson
requires Thai reading, a Thai keyboard, spelling, or handwriting.

## Course shape

- 14 units in four sections
- 41 short lessons and 5 cumulative checkpoints
- 450 authored exercises
- 41 high-frequency vocabulary records and 82 conversational phrase records
- 41 two-person or contextual mini-dialogues with comprehension questions
- 5 checkpoint gates at an 80% first-attempt pass threshold
- 246 audio assets, each planned as a normal and slow recording take
- smart-review nodes in every unit plus per-item scheduling metadata

Every standard lesson introduces exactly three reviewable items: one anchor word
and two complete phrases. It begins with listening recognition, progresses
through meaning recall and phrase construction, includes a conversation response
and dialogue-gist task, and ends with spoken retrieval. From the second lesson
onward, matching practice deliberately brings back an item from the preceding
lesson.

## Listening progression

The path increases listening load without making advanced clips unnaturally slow:

1. isolated anchor word;
2. complete phrase with Romanized choices;
3. short meaning retrieval;
4. connected two-turn exchange;
5. familiar omitted words and conversational particles;
6. gist questions that reward the overall intent;
7. casual reactions, fillers, and playful tone; and
8. safety-critical location and help language under realistic pacing.

The slow control is a separate practice take. It should clarify syllable
boundaries while preserving tones, vowel length, and connected speech.

## Checkpoint and unlock map

The Warm Welcome checkpoint unlocks Units 2–4. The First Travel Conversations
checkpoint unlocks Units 5–8. The Thailand Day and Family checkpoint unlocks
Units 9–11. The Everyday Conversation checkpoint unlocks Units 12–14. The final
course checkpoint is cumulative and unlocks the course-completion achievement.

Checkpoints introduce nothing new. They reuse listening, recognition,
construction, matching, conversation, dialogue, and speaking tasks from the
preceding block. A failed checkpoint keeps its practice history and schedules
weak items for review without unlocking the next block.

## Learner-facing Romanization

Noklingo uses practical Romanized Thai with a visible tone mark on the principal
vowel:

| Tone    | Mark       | Example |
| ------- | ---------- | ------- |
| mid     | none       | `dee`   |
| low     | grave      | `phèt`  |
| falling | circumflex | `mâi`   |
| high    | acute      | `láew`  |
| rising  | caron      | `mǎi`   |

Hyphens divide syllables inside a word; spaces divide spoken words. Long vowels
are written with doubled vowels where useful (`maa`, `rɔ́ɔn`). Aspirated sounds
use `kh`, `ph`, and `th`; unaspirated Thai sounds that English speakers often
mishear use `bp` and `dt`. The course displays `khrap` and beginner-facing `kha`
consistently while retaining the exact Thai source forms.

## Pronunciation priorities

The first goal is intelligibility, not technical transcription. Hints surface
only the contrast likely to change a word or block understanding:

- tone direction on the key syllable;
- short versus long vowel where confusion is likely;
- clipped final consonants;
- unaspirated `bp` and `dt` starts; and
- light, natural polite particles rather than over-stressed endings.

Learners should replay normal speed after slow practice and repeat complete sound
chunks. Thai script can be revealed in Settings but is never used as an answer
requirement.
